How are Dining Options distributed across Boston?

An analysis of dining options, accessibility, and budget-friendly patterns around Northeastern's Boston campus.

Observations 2,847
Features 14
Team Noura Alami, Evelyn Fabel, Colin Jennings
01 Overview

Topic & Research Tasks

Dining is a core component of student life, yet access to afforadable, quality food is unequal across universities, and even cities. This project explores the restaurant landscape of Boston and how their locations are distributed in relation to Northeastern Campus.

We ask three guiding questions: (1) Where are restaurants relative to Northeastern? (2)Does rating correlate with number of ratings? (3) Which neighborhood has the most diverse dining options?

Understanding these patterns can inform university planning decisions, help students make better food choices, and highlight gaps in access that may affect student well-being.
